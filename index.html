    Japanese Interval Walking

    3 minutes slow + 3 minutes fast, repeated 5 times. Developed at Shinshu University, Japan, by Hiroshi Nose & colleagues — ~20 years of research behind it.

    What the studies show

    • Lowers blood pressure ~9/5 mmHg in adults 60+
    • Boosts aerobic fitness (VO₂ peak) ~10% in 5 months
    • Increases leg strength 13–17%
    • Improves blood-sugar control in type 2 diabetes
    • Beats steady-pace walking, minute for minute

    How to pace it

    Fast (3 min): breathing hard, can talk in short phrases, can’t sing. (~70% of peak aerobic capacity.)

    Slow (3 min): easy, conversational. (~40% of peak.)

    Recommended dose

    30 minutes, 4+ days a week. The intervals are what drive the gains — not just total walking time.

    Sources: Nemoto/Nose, Mayo Clin Proc 2007; Karstoft et al., Diabetes Care 2013; Masuki/Nose review 2024. Most data is in middle-aged and older adults; check with a doctor if you have cardiovascular conditions.
